Musings || MCU vs. Comics

Introduction

I am a relatively new fan of Doctor Strange, having only started getting into the character and his story in mid-to-late 2022, but I like to think I have a good grasp on the lore to form my own opinions about it!

I've read a small number of the comics, and whatever I couldn't read I would instead resort to analyses written by long-time Doctor Strange fans. Even with the large number of issues and always-fluctuating canons within the series, I still managed to learn about Stephen, his friends and foes, and the differences between them and their MCU counterparts.

I've enjoyed the comics I've managed to read, but in the end I still prefer Stephen's portrayal in the MCU than in the comics. This isn't to say that the comics are bad by any means, nor am I trying to convince others to forgo the comics. In fact, I highly recommend 616stephenvs*'s' handy reading guide if you want to start reading the comic books!

* Their name is actually Sam, but I want to avoid confusion since they share the same name as my wife, so I'll be referring to them with their Twitter handle. Hopefully, that's ok!

The only reason for writing this is to just to share my opinions, and because I really love talking about Doctor Strange.

Stephen as a Character

I will preface this by saying that Stephen is great in both the comics and the films. However, I personally enjoy how Stephen is handled in the films better for a number of reasons. I've already gushed about him in my short analysis of him as a character in the MCU, but I'd also like to talk about how he compares to his comic book counterpart.

I've mentioned in my analysis that I don't want Stephen to become Sorcerer Supreme in the films for several reasons, the main one being that inheriting the title could potentially prevent him from growing as a character, especially with his already large ego.
