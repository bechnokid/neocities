What is Monster Hunter?

Monster Hunter is an action RPG franchise developed and published by Capcom, with the first game released on the PS2 in 2004. The basic premise of the game is that you have to hunt large monsters with your small cat-like companion called a "Palico". As you progress through the game, you have to hunt monsters and use their parts as armor in order to hunt larger and tougher monsters.

It is very popular in Japan, but never made it as big in Western gaming communities until Monster Hunter: World was released for the PS4 and XBox One. With a massive overhaul in its mechanics, this game became more accessible to casual players.

My first exposure to the franchise was through Animal Crossing: New Leaf - Welcome amiibo in 2016, which is known as New Leaf's first and only major update. This update allowed players to scan all sorts of amiibos to get special items and villagers, including one named Felyne.

However, I didn't think anything of the Monster Hunter collaboration until my wife started playing Animal Crossing: New Horizons in spring 2019. She found the little guy in the villager database and immediately fell in love with him. I was sad to inform her that he wasn't in New Horizons, so she took it upon herself to learn more about the games he, and his fellow feline buddies, came from.

We got the World in summer 2019, and the rest is history!
