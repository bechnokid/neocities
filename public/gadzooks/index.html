Please keep in mind that the majority of the ingredients listed below can only be found in Japanese grocery stores or Asian supermarkets. This particular recipe is missing some ingredients from "traditional" takoyaki recipes, but hey, it's still pretty good!

Takoyaki

Tools

  • Takoyaki griller
  • Silicone brush
  • Mixing bowl
  • Teaspoons and tablespoons
  • Chopsticks

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons of baking powder (NOT BAKING SODA)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of soy sauce
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1/2 teaspoon of dashi powder
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • Octopus sashimi, boiled and cut into small pieces
  • Okonomi sauce
  • Japanese mayonnaise

1) To make the batter, mix the flour, baking powder, salt, and dashi powder into the mixing bowl. Then, add the eggs, soy sauce, and water into the mixture. Mix until you get a smooth consistency.

2) Heat up the takoyaki griller, and use the silicone brush to spread the vegetable oil evenly in each hole.

3) Pour the batter into each hole until they fill up to just below the top of the hole. Be careful not to fill the batter all the way to the top to allow the batter to rise.

4) Place one or two pieces of the octopus into each hole while the batter is cooking. Wait for a few minutes for the batter to grill.

5) Carefully rotate the batter in each hole about 90 degrees using the pair of chopsticks. Continue grilling and rotating until the batter forms a nice and crisp ball.

6) Remove the batter balls from the griller and place them onto a plate. Squeeze some okonomi sauce onto the balls, and then the mayonnaise. Wait for the takoyaki to cool, and enjoy!
